There are many items in Guts and Blackpowder, each being designed for a unique purpose, with most having some background history, this page will briefly explain and showcase the various weapons found in Guts and Blackpowder. More complex breakdowns and guides can be found on each weapon's specific pages. This page will cover firearms, melees, utility, and equippable items.

Fire arms

This section covers all the firearms in Guts and Blackpowder, these ranged weapons can engage zombies safely from a distance, with each shot being capable of dealing a deadly blow to zombies. Each firearm has a headshot multiplier of 900, but Zappers have damage resistance to firearms (with some exceptions).

Single shot weapons

These weapons are traditional single-shot firearms, you fire one shot and then reload, some weapons have unique traits or are specifically designed for certain purposes

Musket

The basic Napoleonic firearm, a powerful and accurate shot, its bayonet can quickly and effictively wipe hordes if mastered.

Carbine

An alternative weapon for the line infantry, trades the bayonet, accuracy, and power per shot for a quick reload, and zombie shoving, each shove deals 10 damage and stuns the zombie. Recommended for players who want the reload of the pistol, but also wants the powerful sidearms of the line infantry.

Pistol

The default weapon for the officer, one of the quickest reloads, but low damage, inaccurate at a far range, good for hunting bombers from close to medium ranges.

Rifle

Multi shot weapons

These firearms have more than one shot, making missing shots less punishing, for increased power, the user may also be able to shoot both barrels in a single burst shot. multi-shot weapons' reloads are on the longer side.

Horse Artillery Pistol

Designed for the British Royal Horse Artillery, the Horse Artillery Pistol is a unique pistol with an attacheable stock and enlongated barrel, akin to that of a carbine, the notable difference is that it is fitted with two barrels and locks, allowing it to fire off two shots, quick reload after firing one shot. But suffers from an uncancelable and long reload if both shots are fired. Alternative weapon for the line infantry class, less accuracy and shot power; no bayonet.

Double Barrel Pistol

An alternative weapon for the officer. Retains the same traits of the Horse Artillery Pistol, but has a quicker reload and is even less accurate and powerful, if used properly, can be considered a direct upgrade to the officer's pistol.

Spread shot weapons

These weapons function like shotguns, firing multiple smaller shots and scattering them using spread, high combined power, but lower damage and penetration per individual shot, making them a powerful horde clearer. These weapons are even more effective in tight funnels or passageways, as shots are almost guaranteed to land despite the varying spread of each weapon.

Blunderbuss

The classic shotgun, high power, but a slower reload and walkspeed. Obtaineable for free for the seaman class, can easily wipe hordes or zombies in a single shot from close to medium ranges.

Nock Gun

Designed by Henry Nock for the Royal Navy, the Nock Gun is a combination of multi and spread shot traits, the Nock Gun has the highest damage per individual shot, with 7 barrels, it suffers from a long reload (if reloading all the barrels). However, the reload can be canceled after an individual shot is rammed down the bore by clicking, saving the shots. The Nock Gun has carbine accuracy, but can easily eradicate hordes at any range, takes a high amount of effort to learn.

Navy Pistol

A quicker and smaller version of the blunderbuss for the seaman, slightly higher damage, but trades accuracy and penetration for speed. Suprisingly fast reload speed, same as the pistol, the weapon also uniquely has the ability to shove zombies, only shared with the carbine. Additionally, the weapon does full damage against Zappers.

Brass Pistol

Functions similar the Navy Pistol, but has a lower range, and a higher reload time, and no shove. The weapon is available for the officer class, Offers a spread-shot option to the officer class.

Fire arms

This section covers all the melees in Guts and Blackpowder, these weapons are generally limited to side-arms that consists of swords or stabbing items, these weapons deal consistent damage but may take a few hits to kill a zombie. All swords have varying damages, ranges, and swing-rates, and some swords can even serve as primaries for some classes.

Sabre

The basic military sword of the time, offers a jack of all trades weapon with no major downsides. Good damage, range, swing speed, and headshot multiplier, the weapon is available on most classes unless they have dedicated melees.

Hand Axe

A small axe that trades out range for damage and a slightly faster swing speed, guaranteed to 2 shot shamblers on all stages of objective maps, and 1 shots shamblers up until wave 5 in endless, great for more skilled players who want to take care of business faster. Available of the line infantry and seaman classes.
